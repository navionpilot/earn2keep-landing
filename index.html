How it works

Two ways to run an event.

Every earn²keep event runs on the same engine — daily challenges, video proof, leaderboard, prizes. The difference is the format: one team raising for a cause, or many teams competing head-to-head for a winner-takes-all pot.

Camp

Single-team fundraiser

Your team, your goal, your earnings.

  • One team. One roster, one goal, one fundraising bar.
  • Each player/participant has a minimum. The organizer sets a per-participant target. Every participant aims for the same number.
  • Bonus points for going over. Pass your minimum and every dollar after that becomes bonus points on the leaderboard.
  • Optional daily challenges. Organizer picks the challenges (push-ups, miles, books, scripture, etc.). Completing them earns door prize eligibility. Skip them and you stay in the fundraiser — you just don't compete for prizes.
  • Door prizes for top performers. 1st, 2nd, and 3rd at the end of the event — set by the organizer.
Best for

Sports teams funding camp gear, youth groups planning a mission trip, classrooms saving for a field trip, gyms backing a charity push.

Tournament

Multi-team showdown

Pay in. Compete. Winning team takes the pot.

  • Multiple teams compete. 4 to 6 teams (or more) each bring their own roster and chase the same prize.
  • Each participant pays a registration fee. You set the amount — typical range is $25–$50 per participant. Every dollar goes into one shared pot.
  • The winning team takes the full pot. Whichever team performs best across the daily challenges wins all of it. That's their fundraising.
  • Same daily-challenge engine. Push-ups, miles, scripture, books, service hours — whatever fits. Challenges are required: completing them is how teams compete.
  • Built-in peer motivation. Everyone on the team has real money on the line. Real stakes mean real effort from everyone.
Best for

League-wide season showdowns, district tournaments, multi-gym competitions, regional ministry events, church-vs-church challenges.

For Supporters

Just direct support for a cause that matters to them. (Tournaments fund themselves through participant registration fees — no external supporters needed.)
