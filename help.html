Help & FAQ

Quick answers to the questions we hear most. Can't find what you're looking for? Email us at support@earn2keep.com — a real person reads every message.

For Organizers

What does it cost to run a fundraiser?
$99 per Mini-Camp (for small groups or first-time fundraisers), $149 per Camp (standard single-team), or $349 per Tournament (multi-team competition). Mini-Camp and Camp events also include standard payment processing on donations: 3.5% + $0.30 per donation, which covers Stripe — we don't add a markup on top. Tournaments have no percentage fee at all (Stripe handles registration processing directly). We don't take a cut of your fundraising. No hidden setup fees, no per-participant charges.
What's the difference between a Camp and a Tournament?
Camp is for a single team raising money for a cause. Participants share their personal QR codes and links with family, friends, and community supporters — anyone can donate any amount via Stripe, and the money goes to your organization. Daily challenges (push-ups, scripture, miles, etc.) are optional: participants who complete them earn eligibility for door prizes you set, but skipping them still keeps you in the fundraiser.

Tournament is for multiple teams competing head-to-head. Each participant pays a Registration Fee you set (typical: $25–$50 per participant). All registration fees go into one shared pot. Daily challenges are required in a Tournament — completing them is how teams compete. The winning team takes the full pot at the end of the event. There are no external supporters in a Tournament — registration fees are the funding mechanism.
How long can an event run?
Up to 30 days. Most groups find that 2–3 weeks is the sweet spot — long enough for momentum, short enough to keep participants engaged.
Can I run a fundraiser if I'm not a sports coach?
Yes. We support eight organizer roles: coach, parent, teacher, youth pastor, scout leader, gym owner/instructor, organization director, and “anyone else who leads a group.” The challenges adapt — push-ups for sports teams, scripture memorization for youth groups, books read for classrooms, miles run for cross-country, service hours for scouts, etc.
What if I don't see a challenge that fits my group?
You can create custom challenges from the challenge library. Pick the unit (reps, miles, minutes, hours, books, verses, etc.), write a brief description, and you're set. We're also always open to adding more templates — email us with what you'd like to see.
How do participants submit their work?
Each participant gets a personal link via email. They tap it, log in (no app store install needed — it's a web app), pick a challenge, record a short video, and submit. The submission lands in your review queue. AI verifies it automatically when possible, and you approve, reject, or adjust anything AI can't confirm with one tap. Approved submissions roll up to the leaderboard.
How do supporters donate to my Camp?
We generate a unique QR code and personal link for each participant. You can download printable flyers (with their name, photo, and code) or share the codes digitally. Supporters scan, pick any dollar amount, and pay through Stripe. The money goes to your organization (minus the 3.5% + $0.30 per donation fee that covers Stripe payment processing). Most groups have participants share with parents, grandparents, neighbors, coworkers, and church friends — the natural circles where they'd normally sell candy bars.

Note: this applies to Camps. Tournaments fund themselves through participant Registration Fees — no external supporters.
When does the money arrive?
For a Camp, supporter donations are processed via Stripe at the moment each donation is made. For a Tournament, participant Registration Fees are collected up front when each participant registers, and the winning team receives the full pot at the end of the event. Funds settle to your organization's payout account on Stripe's standard schedule (typically 2 business days after each transaction is processed).
What if a participant records something wrong or fakes a rep count?
That's exactly why every submission is video-verified. AI does a first-pass check, and you review anything it can't confirm. If something looks off — bad camera angle, claimed 50 reps but the video only shows 20 — you can adjust the count or reject with a note. Participants see your feedback and can re-record.

For Players

Do I need to download an app?
No. earn²keep runs in any browser on any phone. Your organizer sends you a link, you tap it, and you're in. You can optionally save it to your home screen for one-tap access, but it's never required.
What do I actually do?
Your organizer picks the challenges (push-ups, scripture, books, miles, whatever). You record a short video of yourself doing the activity, tell the app how many reps you did, and submit. AI verifies it when possible, and your organizer reviews anything AI can't confirm. Approved submissions earn points on the leaderboard — and in a Camp, your supporters donate to back your cause.
How private is my video?
Your submission videos are private to you and your organizer. They're not posted publicly, not shown on a feed, not shareable as links. Only people with explicit access to your organization's review queue can see them.
I'm under 13. Can I use earn²keep?
With a parent or guardian's permission, yes. We follow COPPA rules for users under 13 — see our Children's Privacy notice for details. Your organizer (or your parent) will be the one managing your account on the back end.

For Supporters (Camp Only)

How do I donate?
Scan the QR code (or open the link) the participant shared with you. Pick any dollar amount that feels right. Enter your payment info. That's it. We'll send you a receipt and you can follow along as the participant earns badges, completes challenges, and climbs the leaderboard.
When am I charged?
You're charged the moment you confirm your donation. One-time, no subscription, no recurring billing.
Can I get a refund?
Yes — see our refund policy. Donations are refundable within 30 days for any reason. After 30 days, refunds are case-by-case. Email support@earn2keep.com and we'll work with you.
Is my donation tax-deductible?
Depends on whether the organization you're supporting is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit. earn²keep itself isn't a charity — we're the platform. If you need tax documentation, ask the organization you donated to directly.

Still stuck?

Email support@earn2keep.com and tell us what's going on. We aim to respond within one business day. Include a screenshot if you can — it makes troubleshooting much faster.
